Why Hemp?

Carbon Warrior

Hemp Is A Carbon Warrior. Global warming is real and what is even more real, is the carbon contribution of the fashion industry to this effect. ... Switching to Hemp Clothing would mean planting more Hemp plants which help the process of CO2 absorption from the atmosphere at a faster pace as compared to other trees.

Water Saviour

Hemp production doesn't consume a lot of water, unlike cotton. It needs half the amount of water. Thus, it helps in tackling the scarcity of water. It is a strong plant and grows quicker than cotton as well.

Land Management

This incredible plant is extremely sustainable. It takes nearly 50per cent less land for commercial production in comparison to cotton. This environment saviour plant is used to not just eliminate pesticides effects, soil remediation, weeding fields, and bio-absorption of heavy metals and contaminants but also to control atmospheric CO2.

Antimicrobial

Durable

Hemp fiber is one of the strongest and most durable of all natural textile fibers. ... Hemp is also naturally resistant to mold and ultraviolet light. Due to the porous nature of the fiber, hemp is more water absorbent, and will dye and retain its color better than any fabric including cotton.

Soften With Time

Each time hemp is washed it constantly reveals new surfaces, usually becoming softer with use. Given reasonable care it will render a lifetime of service.

UV Resistant

Hemp has a high resistance to ultraviolet light; it will not fade or disintegrate from sunlight as quickly as other natural fibers. ... Finally, any product made of hemp is fully biodegradable and easily recyclable.
